Self-serve app promotion tool
Publishers and agencies can use Roku's self-serve app promotion tool to programmatically purchase Roku home screen banner ads, Roku screensaver ads, and video ads in order to promote apps, increase viewership, and reach users on the Roku platform. This single tool enables the targeting of viewers with Roku's exclusive audience data, launching ad campaigns to convert viewers, and then measuring and optimizing campaign performance in real-time.
Getting started
To sign up for Roku's self-serve app promotion tool, follow these steps:
Go to admanager.roku.com , and then click Sign up .

Select one of the following account types:

Direct Advertiser . You book ads for your own app.
Agency . You book ads for apps on behalf of publishers.

Enter your full name, company name, and company email address, and then select the currency in which you will purchase ads. Once you create your account, the currency cannot be changed.

Select the first two checkboxes to agree to the Terms and Conditions and Roku Privacy Policy and confirm that you are authorized to accept the terms. Optionally, select the last check box to receive promotional and informational emails from the tool.

You will receive a confirmation email that enables you to enter your password for the tool and complete the registration.

Dashboard
When you sign in to the tool, the dashboard displays the following information:
Lifetime statistics . The total number of clients (agencies only), campaigns (active, ready, inactive), ad impressions, app installs, budget, and spend.

Active campaigns performance . The number of ad impressions or app installs for the ad campaigns that are currently running from over the last 3, 7 (default), 14, or 30 days.

Campaigns starting soon . The name, flight dates, creative status, campaign status, and budget for each upcoming ad campaign.

Top campaigns . The completed campaigns that delivered the most ad impressions or app installs.

Top clients (agencies only). The clients whose campaigns delivered the most ad impressions or app installs.

Creating new ad campaigns
To create a new ad campaign, follow these steps:
Click New campaign .

In the Campaign name panel, enter a maximum 100-character name, which may contain alphanumeric and special characters (@, #, &, -, _, ', ( ), %, $, *).

In the Placement panel, do the following: a. Under Creative type , select the type of ad creative to run: Display . Run a home screen banner ad or screensaver ad promoting an app published on the Roku platform. This is the default. Video . Run a 15-second or 30-second video ad, which is included in an ad pod played during an ad break in content. b. Under Market , select in which country to target users with the ad. Display ads may be run in the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Mexico, and Brazil. Video ads may be run in the United States, United Kingdom, and Canada. c. Under Campaign type , select the type of metric to be used for the campaign: CPI (cost per install; for display ads only) or CPM (cost per 1,000 impressions; for display or video campaigns). d. If you are creating a display ad, click Select placement , and then select whether you are creating a home screen or screensaver ad.

In the My channel panel, click Select channel and then do the following: a. From the Channel list, select the name or ID of the app to be advertised. The list of apps is automatically filtered as you type (2-character minimum). If your app was recently published, it may take up to 1 business week for your app to be included in the list. b. From the Channel category list, select one of the pre-defined categories (for example, general viewing, sports, or horror). You can create multiple listings to use the same app with different categories. For example, your app may typically fall into "General Viewing"; however, if you are promoting a horror movie, you can use the "Horror" app category. c. Click Save . d. The next time you create an ad, the selected app will be included in the Recently Used list and can be chosen with a single click. You can add other apps by clicking New Channel ; you can delete an app by clicking Manage , and then clicking the garbage can icon.

In the Targeting options panel, you can optionally select the audience segments (CPM campaigns only) and geographic data to be used for advanced targeting. In order for viewers to be targeted with an ad, they need to be one of the selected audience segments AND in one of the selected locations. a. Under Audience segment (for CPM ad campaigns only; this option is not available for CPI campaigns), add one or more segments to your target audience in the Include box. You can select segments from the following categories: genres, sports, tentpole events, viewership, and age & gender. You can also add one or more of these segments in the Exclude box. Users only need to match one of the selected segments to meet the Include or Exclude criteria (OR logic). For example, to targets viewers who like sports, but are not 65 or older, you could include the Sports Enthusiasts segment and exclude the Adults 65+ segment. b. Under Geography , select one or more locations (cities, countries, counties, designated marketing areas (DMAs), municipalities, postal [zip] codes, or states). The list of locations is automatically filtered as you type (2-character minimum). If you using postal codes, it is recommended that you enter a minimum of 10. Alternatively, you can click Advanced , select a location type, enter a delimiter-separated list of locations (for example, Los Angeles, Cincinnati, Philadelphia or California, Ohio, Pennsylvania), click Next , and then click Finish . You can use a vertical bar, comma, semicolon, or newline as the delimiter between each location. c. Under Creative optimization , keep the Optimize creative rotation toggle enabled to prioritize creative delivery by CTR (clickthrough rate). This means that the creatives with the highest CTRs are delivered more frequently than those with lower CTRs. Disable the toggle to rotate the creatives evenly at random.

In the Budget and Schedule panel, enter the budget and flight dates of the ad campaign: a. Enter the amount of money to spend on the ad campaign, or enter the target number of installs or impressions. Entering one field automatically updates the other. For example, entering a budget updates the installs and impressions by dividing the budget by the current CPI or CPM. b. In the Start date and End date boxes, enter the date and time when the ad campaign starts and stops running. c. In the Time Zone box, select the time zone on which the selected flight date-times are based. The list of time zones is automatically filtered as you type (2-character minimum). For example, you can type "08" or "Pacific" to select "(UTC-08:00) Pacific Time (US & Canada)".

In the Creatives panel, select the ad creative to be used. You can upload a new ad creative or select a previously approved one. See the Roku Display Ad Creative Guidelines to ensure your creative meets all the requirements and for best practices in creating a successful ad. a. To upload a creative, click Upload creative . b. In the Creative name box, enter a maximum 100-character name, which may contain alphanumeric and special characters (@, #, &, -, _, ', ( ), %, $, *). c. Under Image , either drag and drop or click and upload a 525 x 735 JPEG, JPG, or PNG. The maximum file size is 150 KB (larger files are automatically compressed). Images not meeting the required dimensions must be edited in the crop tool. d. Under Click action , select whether clicking the display ad opens the app home page or deep links to specific content in the app. Open your app . Clicking the display ad launches the app home page. Open specific content . Clicking the display ad deep links to the content. Enter the contentId and mediaType of the content in the Deep Link Parameters box. The contentId uniquely identifies content in an app; the mediaType specifies how an app should behave when receiving a deep link request . You can use the Deep Linking Tester to verify that the deep link works. Syntax : contentid= contentId &mediatype= mediaType Example : contentid=roku-streaming-overview&mediatype=movie e. Click Save . The ad creative will be reviewed for compliance with Roku’s Ad Policies This review is typically completed within three business days. This review should be factored into your schedule for launching the campaign. f. When you subsequently create ads, you can click Use from library in the Creatives panel, and then select your previously approved creatives. If you use an already approved creative from the library, it does not need to be approved again (the campaign begins delivering on the start date/time).

Review the campaign settings from the pane on the right. You can click a header to go to a setting and edit it. When you are finished reviewing your campaign and making adjustments, click Add to cart .

Click Continue to cart to review your order, or click Build another campaign and repeat steps 1-9. You can add multiple campaigns to the cart and check out at the same time.

In the Cart page, review the ad campaigns you plan on running. For each campaign you have created, this page summarizes the booked amount of installs or impressions, flight dates, and total purchase price. You can edit, delete, or preview the individual ad campaigns in the cart. Click Proceed to checkout . If you have received a promo code, enter it at checkout to receive a discount on your booking.

In the Bookings page, enter your billing information and tax ID or other tax number identifier (if you don't have one, select the "I don't have a tax..." check box), click Next , and then click Proceed to checkout .

In the Payment Details page, enter your method of payment. Optionally, select the Save card for future use checkbox to use the entered method of payment for subsequent purchases. Click the I agree to the Payment Terms & Conditions check box, and then click Complete purchase .

Campaigns
The Campaigns dashboard summarizes the ad campaigns in your account. For each campaign, it lists the unique ID, name, status, booked amount, impressions and installs delivered, and current spend, and budget. You can sort the campaigns by the column headers; filter them by type, status, creative name, and creative status; and search for specific reports. You can also export the campaigns summary to an .XLS file for a specific date range.
You can click anywhere in a row to edit, copy, pause/resume, and change the creative for a campaign.
Editing campaigns
To update the the name, targeting, or creative of an ad campaign, follow these steps:
Click anywhere in the row for the campaign to be updated.

The Campaign details page summarizes the current performance/goal and current spend/budget for the campaign.

Click Edit , update the settings in the Campaign Edit details page, and then click Save .

Copying campaigns
To create a new ad campaign from the template provided by an existing one, follow these steps:
Click anywhere in the row for the campaign to be copied.

In the Campaign details page, click Copy .

In the Campaign Edit details page, update the campaign's name, placement, app, targeting, budget and scheduling, and creative, and then pay for it following the steps in Creating an ad campaign .

Pausing campaigns
To pause/resume an ad campaign, follow these steps:
Click anywhere in the row for the campaign to be paused/resumed. A campaign must have already been approved to be paused.

In the Campaign details page, click Pause . You can then click Resume to restart the ad campaign later.

Uploading creatives
To change the creative for an ad campaign, follow these steps:
Click anywhere in the row for the campaign to be updated with a new creative.

In the Campaign details page under Creatives , click Upload creative .

Provide the name, image, and click action for the creative following step 7 in Creating new ad campaigns .

Reports
The Reports dashboard enables publishers and agencies to track and analyze the status and performance of their ad campaigns. It enables the creation of custom reports that can be downloaded to an .XLS file immediately or scheduled for regular email delivery. The main dashboard lists the previously saved report. You can sort the saved reports by the column headers, and search for specific reports.
Creating and downloading a report
To create a one-time report and download it as an .XLS file, follow these steps:
Click Create New .

In the New statistic report page, enter the following information: a. Report name . The name of the report (the default name is Roku_Ad_Manager_report_DD-month-YYYY). b. Date range . Enter the start and end dates for which the campaign statistics are to be provided.

Keep the Save the report to the list check box selected to add it to the Reports dashboard.
Click Download and save .

Scheduling report delivery
To create a recurring report and schedule its email delivery, follow these steps:
From the Reports dashboard, click Schedule report delivery .

Click Create New .

In the New statistic report page, enter the following information: a. Report name . The name of the report (the default name is Roku_Ad_Manager_report_DD-month-YYYY). b. Email to receive report . Enter the email addresses of the individuals to receive the report. c. Date range . Enter the start and end dates for which the campaign statistics are to be provided. d. Delivery frequency . Select how often to email the report to the listed recipients: Daily , First day of the week (weekly), or First day of the month (monthly).
Click Create . The listed recipient will receive the campaign report as an .XLS attachment via an email notification, which is delivered regularly according to the specified frequency.

User profile
You can manage your account and view your bookings by clicking the User Profile icon in the upper right-hand corner of the UI.
Under Account , you can edit your company information and reset your password, and click Payment information to add or update your method of payment for your bookings.

Under Bookings , you can view a list of your booked ad campaigns. For each booking, the unique id, payment status, booking date, booking budget, total, booking spend, and final total is included. You can export your bookings for a specific date range to an .XLS file.

Activity notifications
You can view your activity notifications by clicking the Notification icon in the upper right-hand corner of the UI.
